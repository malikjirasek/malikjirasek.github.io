I am a Master's student in physics at the University of Tübingen with a focus on quantum sensing and open quantum systems. My work explores novel sensing protocols using driven-dissipative many-body quantum systems that promise drastically higher precision than their classical counterparts. I am particularly interested in the complex behavior in these many-body settings that leads to phenomena such as phase transitions and spontaneous symmetry breaking.

Latest Publication

2025 preprint at arXiv · 2511.23416 [quant-ph]

The Boundary Time Crystal as a light source for quantum enhanced sensing beyond the Heisenberg Limit

Jirasek, M., Lesanovsky, I., Cabot, A.

We propose the implementation of driven-dissipative many-body quantum systems as light sources in quantum sensing applications. We demonstrate their advantage by simulating the precision of sensing optical phases with a so called Boundary Time Crystal as a light source.
